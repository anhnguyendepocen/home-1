Curriculum Vitae

I am an associate professor at the Department of Economics, Harvard University.
My research interests include:
  • Econometric methods for the analysis of economic inequality
  • Experimental design
  • Publication bias
  • The role of economic theory in estimation
  • Machine learning, empirical Bayes, statistical decision theory
  • Optimal policy choice and econometrics
  • Identification and parameters of interest in microeconometrics
On this webpage you will find:
  • Manuscripts and presentation slides for my published and unpublished papers.
  • Syllabi, slides, and readers for my classes, and an open online textbook on economic inequality.
  • Source-code and interactive apps for optimal experimental design, optimal policy choice using machine learning, simulating wealth taxes, etc.
  • Links to my op-eds.
